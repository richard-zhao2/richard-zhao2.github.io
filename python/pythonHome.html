Computer Science with Python

Hi, my name's Richard and this is a very simple introduction to computer science.

One of the main issues I have with a lot of online resources is that they're usually glorified syntax references. Rarely do I find sources that develop problem-solving skills, and the ones that do often focus very heavily on that alone, so I made this site in hopes that it'll help bridge the gap between knowing a bunch of syntax and knowing how to use that knowledge to solve problems.

Another thing I hate about a lot of online resources is that they always use videos(which are nice in their own way) but sometimes I want to power through documentation and learn quickly instead of listening to poorly written jokes for an extra 3 minutes. Other times I like to watch a video when I need to go slower. Sometimes I like to do practice problems to brush up on skills, and other times I just want a refresher. What usually ends up happening is that I don't get to choose which style I want to learn with, so I split up content and practice problems.

I added a solutions section to give a sample solution of the problems. A lot of websites don't like giving you solutions in hopes that it "forces" you to learn. What usually ends up happening is I get mad and just leave the site, having learned nothing.

I like starting off with Python because it's an easy language to pickup, but language choice doesn't really matter in the end.
